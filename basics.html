THE RIGHT PIXEL CAN UNLOCK CHANGE

DESIGN ACTIVISM USES the design process to facilitate positive change in the world. Design activists work with communities and interdisciplinary teams to tackle socioeconomic, political, and environmental causes. [1]

“I believe that we are at a global turning point. Design now has to be for social good. We require a new action frame.”

Victor Margolin, 2002

Design Historian

THE 6 PRINCIPLES OF DESIGN ACTIVISM

INTERDISCIPLINARY

Design activism does not claim expertise on any topic. Instead, it offers a creative and design-based framework for cross-disciplinary professionals to collaborate and further solutions. [2]

COMMUNITY-DRIVEN

Design activism uplifts the human agency of historically marginalized groups. It opens platforms for collaboration rather than imposition. It “works with” rather than “on behalf of.”[3]

PROBLEM-FOCUSED

Before offering solutions, design activists focus on understanding the problem in depth. They aim to not only ask questions, but to discover the right questions to ask. [3]

ITERATIVE

Design activism engages in continuous research. It understands that problems do not end when solved; they require monitoring and iteration. [3]

TRANSFORMATIVE

Fueled by borderless imagination, design activists create a platform to identify new ways, new systems, and structures to improve livelihood and well-being. [2]

SOCIALLY ORIENTED

Design activists focus on generating projects that benefit collective and social ends instead of tackling predominantly commercial objectives. [5]

3 DIFFERENT TYPES OF DESIGN ACTIVISTS

SOLVER

A designer who lends their skills to help a community address a specific problem. [2]

CAMPAGINER

A designer who helps a community call attention to a problem unrecognized by authorities and assist by creating an awareness campaign. [2]

FORECASTER

A designer who foresees a potential issue and uses design to avoid the creation of the problem. [2]
