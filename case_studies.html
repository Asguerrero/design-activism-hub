QUEERING THE MAP

Queering the map is a platform that facilitates the digital archival of LBTQ+ stories in relation to geography. By placing markers in an interactive world map, queer can record their life experiences in specific physical spaces. This global mapping aims to showcase how bonds can be built across borders and cultures, revealing the universality of stories.

Queering the map’s activism type qualifies as “problem-solving,” and it fulfills the six fundamental principles of design activism.

DESIGNING
MOTHERHOOD

Designing motherhood is an extensive research initiative that studies human reproduction through the lens of design. Its central premise is that: while birth often brings great joy, making babies has been a complex enterprise led by patriarchal structures. By looking at iconic, archaic, and modern designed objects surrounding pregnancy and childbirth, this initiative has redefined the relationships between people and babies during the past century.

DEMOTECH

Based in the Netherlands, Demotech is an organization that focuses on re-designing concepts, tools, methods, and ideas to make them accessible and affordable to everybody in the world. Through research and testing, they craft open-source manuals for building artifacts that can solve the daily challenges of people facing economic difficulties. Their water and sanitation systems and energy generation structures are regarded worldwide as examples of innovative solutions to develop a sustainable future.

VIOLET PROTEST

Ann Morton

The Violet Protest is a collective attempt to deliver 50 hand-made ​textile squares to every member of the US Congress. Whether they are embroidery or crochet, the violet pieces (representing the literal merging of red and blue) suggest congresspeople to collaborate beyond ideological polarization peacefully. The square crafts will be delivered to the 117th Congress by late 2021.

DESIGN FOR A
HAPPY WORLD

Design For A Happy World is a virtual survey that helps designers determine what projects to accept or decline based on environmental and social factors. It considers clients’ association with the fossil fuels industry, animal cruelty, warfare, modern slavery, land degradation, etc., to assess whether it is worth getting involved with them.

LANDSCAPING
